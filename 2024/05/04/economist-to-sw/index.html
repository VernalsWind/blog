印度大选
This week we put India on our cover. Voting is under way（大选正在进行） and in just six weeks（仅仅6周以后） Narendra Modi is expected to（将很有期望） win a third term（赢得第三任期） as prime minister（总理、首相，最重要的prime，minister部长）, cementing（cement水泥） his status as the country’s most important leader since Jawaharlal Nehru.（这里用水泥表达巩固，直接惊艳到我了，果然是英国人，说英语就是比美国人更有灵气，所以决定分享给你了）

Our coverage focuses on India’s economy and at its heart is a special report by two of our correspondents(前线记者，通讯员), Arjun Ramani and Thomas Easton. Many Indians support Mr Modi because they sense that under him India has grown more prosperous and that it has become a force （一股强大力量，或者翻译成大国）in the world. We agree that Mr Modi’s formula（公式，引申义方案、方针） for growth is working（有效，起作用）—up to a point （在一定程度上，有点，ps:英国人还是太傲慢了哈哈）. But we also question （质疑）whether India’s success can last（持续） and whether it depends on his remaining in power.（翻译不出来了，懂得都懂，英语里用名词（比如这里的his remaining）就是比用动词高级，特别是学术论文）

Today Mr Modi dominates India more than ever（比过去任何时刻都……dominant）. As much as a vote can be a foregone conclusion (选举结果一如所料)in a democracy, his victory is assured. The success of this tea-seller’s son reflects his political skill and the potency （威力，动词potent）of his Hindu-nationalist ideology（印度民族主义意识形态） (but also his erosion of democratic institutions). （他对民主体制的erosion磨损和腐蚀）Naturally, therefore,（写作可以用，因此） our thoughts turned to him. In one early design we had Mr Modi pointing the way ahead, and in another posing as India’s muscle-bound(肌肉粗大僵硬) strongman.

Yet precisely because we can foresee(预料) putting Mr Modi on the cover in his moment of triumph(胜利) in June, we wanted something different this week.

An image showing his fist(拳头) in an arrow combined a nod to Mr Modi’s strongman politics with a symbol of India’s growth. But it risked signalling that the intolerant(无法忍受的) and chauvinistic（大国沙文主义的） parts of Mr Modi’s style are what lie behind his success. That isn’t quite right. His most fruitful policies（政策） draw on the liberal agenda that emerged in India in the 1990s and 2000s. Where his authority has counted has been in his determination to force through stalled reforms, personally overseeing key decisions and browbeating laggards and opponents in the bureaucracy.

A tiger climbing a bar chart was better. India’s economy is expanding at an annual rate of 6-7%, which makes it the fastest-growing of any big country—though, in truth, that is a continuation of long-run(长期运行) trends rather than（而不是） a dramatic acceleration.（加速） A massive programme of infrastructure knits (编织)together a vast single market: India has 149 airports, double the number a decade ago(10年前的两倍), and is adding 10,000km of roads and 15gw of solar-energy capacity（太阳能容量） a year. Rising wealth means more geopolitical heft（重量，引申为影响力）. Both Joe Biden and Donald Trump have used their presidencies to court(取悦) it without disputing(和argue异曲同工，本意为争辩，这里为主张) that it should remain broadly non-aligned.（不结盟国家）

However, this illustration（插图） was too neutral. We wanted the cover both to be more lively and also to hint at our reservations.

One thought was to show India’s growing strength by having the eight-armed goddess often known as Durga supporting a great weight. But, understandably, people often resent their gods and goddesses being used to make a point.（make a point重视） A monster-truck version of a tuk-tuk managed to be lively without breaking taboos—but it was still missing our reservations. 

India is developing at a time of stagnating goods trade and factory automation. In a working-age population of 1bn（10亿,1 billion）, only 100m（1亿100 million） or so have formal jobs. Most of the rest(剩下的) are stuck in casual work or joblessness. India therefore needs to pioneer（使……成为先锋，优先推动） a new model for growth.

We lit upon two versions of the lotus, India’s national flower, as a symbol for this distinctive model（卓绝的榜样）. In one, growth was represented by a bar chart（条形图）, poking(捅、戳) stamen-like（雄蕊状） out of the flower.（你看那个封面，莲花是从低到高排列的，就像条形图一样） In the other a row of lotus stems showed India’s rising prosperity. Some of us worried that a stylised version of the lotus flower is also the logo of the Bharatiya Janata Party(印度人民党), or bjp, which is Mr Modi’s party. But, given the likelihood(可能性) of his victory, that was a strength of this idea, not a weakness.

We think that India could continue to grow, with an even bigger IT sector（部门） and a cluster（簇、聚集） of export industries. An efficient, single domestic market would raise productivity and well-targeted welfare could help those who fall behind. For this, India would have to transform education and agriculture, and enable migration from the populous（人口稠密） north to big southern and western cities. However, we worry that Mr Modi and his party (党派，这里指印度人民党)have not grappled with the magnitude of the challenge ahead.
That message came across better with the cover showing several flowers—especially if we were to make the stem on the tallest lotus look less steady. To create a new reform agenda and foster a thriving knowledge economy(蓬勃发展的知识经济), Mr Modi will have to temper his autocratic impulses. To attract more local and foreign investment and to find a growth-minded successor, his party will need to curb （名词马路沿*We sat on the curb eating our ice cream*，动词猜测是约束）its chauvinistic （大国沙文主义，比如斯大林和勃列日涅夫时期的苏联老大哥）politics. If not, Mr Modi’s mission （使命）of national renewal will（民族复兴的宏图大志） not live up to （必背短语）its promise.
